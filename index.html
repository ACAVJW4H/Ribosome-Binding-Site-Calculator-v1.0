The Ribosome Binding Site (RBS) Calculator by Howard Salis and Chris Voigt

The Ribosome Binding Site (RBS) Calculator can predict the translation initiation rate of a protein coding sequence in bacteria and design synthetic RBS sequences to rationally control the translation initiation rate.

Microbial engineering often requires fine control over protein expression; for example, to connect genetic circuits or control flux through a metabolic pathway. We have developed a predictive design method for synthetic ribosome binding sites (the 'RBS Calculator') that enables the rational control of a proteinâ€™s production rate on a proportional scale. Experimental validation of over 100 predictions in Escherichia coli shows that the method is accurate to within a factor of 2.3 over a range of 100,000-fold. The design method also correctly predicts that reusing a ribosome binding site sequence in different genetic contexts can result in different protein expression levels. The proposed forward engineering approach will accelerate the construction and systematic optimization of large genetic systems.

For technical details, please read the scientific article:

H.M. Salis, E.A. Mirsky, C.A. Voigt, "Automated Design of Synthetic Ribosome Binding Sites to Precisely Control the Protein Expression Level", Nature Biotechnology, 2009

Dependencies

1) Either the NuPACK software suite, http://nupack.org or the ViennaRNA software suite, http://www.tbi.univie.ac.at/RNA/

2) Biopython (optional)

Install

1) Install NuPACK or ViennaRNA and add their respective /bin directories to the PATH.

2) Install Biopython (optional)

3) Test run the RBS Calculator by typing 'python RBS_Calculator.py' or 'python RBS_MC_Design.py' or use the command line interfaces.
